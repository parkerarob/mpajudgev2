                      Concert Band MPA: Key Information

                      Eligibility

                      • Director must be a current NCMEA member.
                      • The director of record must register students and be present at the event.
                      • Students must be regularly enrolled in band at the school.

                      Performance Format

                      • Bands may participate for rating or comments only.
                      • Comments-only groups may choose whether to sight-read.
                      • Comments-only groups do not receive a rating.

                      Music Requirements

                      • Perform 1 march and 2 selections from the current NCBA approved list.
                      • A Grade VI masterwork may be used in place of the 2 listed selections when permitted.
                      • Selections must come from the same grade level or two adjacent grade levels.
                      • Use of unapproved music results in comments only and no rating.

                      Required Materials

                      • Bring 3 conductor's scores for each concert selection.
                      • All scores must have measure numbers.
                      • Copied scores require publisher permission.
                      • E-print scores require proof of purchase.
                      • Submit instrumentation and 4-year repertoire history at least 1 hour before performance.

                      Time Limits

                      Performance time includes staging and performance.

                      Group Warm-Up Performance
                      Groups I–II25 min25 min
                      Groups III–IV30 min30 min
                      Group V35 min35 min
                      Group VI40 min40 min

                      Additional time must be approved at least 30 days in advance.

                      Sight-Reading Rules

                      • Required for Grades II–VI. Grade I does not sight-read.
                      • During prep, students may clap, sing, count aloud, and finger. Students may not play.
                      • One brief warm-up scale is allowed before reading.
                      • Only one director may instruct in the sight-reading room.
                      Grade Prep Time
                      Grades II–IV5 minutes
                      Grade V6 minutes
                      Grade VI7 minutes

                      Sight-Reading Preparation

                      Reminders

                      • Assign percussion parts before arrival.
                      • Pre-plan all wind part splits and section coverage.
                      • Practice sight-reading at your current registered MPA level.

                      Auxiliary Percussion by Grade

                      Grade / UIL Level Possible Players
                      Grade 2 / UIL Level 21 player could play both Triangle and Crash Cymbals.
                      Grade 3 / UIL Level 32 players minimum — Crash Cymbals, Suspended Cymbal, and Triangle.
                      Grade 4 / UIL Level 42 players minimum — Crash Cymbals, Triangle, and Suspended Cymbal.
                      Grade 5 / UIL Level 51 player could play both Crash Cymbals and Suspended Cymbals.
                      Grade 6 / UIL Level 62 players — Wood Block, Suspended Cymbal, and Crash Cymbals.
